GOAL
We're collecting negative app-store reviews and extracting product-level insights. We need:
1. The CORRECT category boundaries (not too many, not redundant, mappable to real product domains).
2. Per category, the BEST apps to analyze — deduplicated (same brand multi-listed) and biased toward apps likely to yield rich product insights (mainstream / large user base / common pain patterns).

CURATION RULES

1. **Within-category dedup**: If 3+ listings share a brand prefix (QuickBooks, Xero, Photo Lab, Picsart) or come from the same developer with very similar names, collapse them into ONE canonicalApp with all ids. Use the highest-review listing as primaryId. Sub-products (e.g. "Workforce" vs "Self-Employed") that target genuinely different use cases CAN stay separate IF distinct enough — but err on the side of merging.

2. **Cross-category merges**: 
   - "ai-chat" vs "ai-chatbot-assistant" — these are the same domain, merge.
   - "fitness-workout" vs "gym-fitness-apps" — likely overlap, evaluate.
   - "books-reading" vs "language-exchange-apps" — likely distinct, don't merge.
   - "kids-learning" vs "language-kids-apps" — overlap, evaluate.
   - "finance-personal" vs "budget-tracking-apps" — likely overlap, evaluate.
   - "smb-invoicing" vs "accounting-software" — likely overlap for the small-biz angle, evaluate.
   - "ai-face-photo" vs "ai-video-creation" vs "ai-generators" — three flavors of generative AI media, evaluate.
   - Look for other obvious overlaps. Be honest — if two categories have heavily overlapping apps, merge them.

3. **Drop categories** that are: too small to give signal (under ~3 unique brands after dedup), too niche to matter, or just don't form a coherent product category. Examples: "ticketing-events" (4 apps), "email-apps" (2 apps), "language-exchange-apps" (2 apps), "ai-video-creation" (2 apps). Drop unless they're strategically interesting.

4. **Re-categorize misclassified apps**: Sometimes an app sits in the wrong category. If you see one, note it in canonicalApps with a moved-from note in rationale.

5. **Priority** = how rich the insights will be:
   - high = mainstream consumer app domain with rich app-store complaint patterns (sleep, fitness, finance, AI, dating, period-tracking)
   - medium = useful but more niche (HR, specific utilities, specific kids apps)
   - low = small or contractual (mobile-carrier apps, airline apps, government services, theme parks) — keep but flag

6. **Leaders inside a category**: Mark `isLeader: true` for the 2-5 most insight-valuable apps in each category (large user base, frequent updates, deep product surface area). Keep the others as long-tail.

7. **Aim for ~50-70 final categories** after merges/drops — that's a sweet spot between coverage and signal density. Don't be afraid to merge.

TARGET QUALITY
- Russian + English names should be precise (not "Apps for X" — just "X").
- Kicker should describe what's INSIDE the category in one line so a reader knows what kinds of apps they'll see.
- Don't invent apps not in the input. Only consolidate / re-label / move what's already there.
